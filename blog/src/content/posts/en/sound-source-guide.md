## What Are Sound Sources?

Sound sources determine the tone of the guide notes played during scale practice.

![Output settings screen](/images/en/06_settings_audio_output.png)

Choose from 8 different sources based on your preferences and practice goals.

## 8 Sound Sources

### Piano Family

#### Acoustic Grand Piano (Default)

Classic piano sound.

- **Characteristics**: Clear and easy to hear
- **Best For**: All-around practice
- **Recommendation**: ★★★★★

When in doubt, choose this. Clear pitch makes it suitable for any practice.

#### Electric Piano

Soft electric piano tone.

- **Characteristics**: Warm sound
- **Best For**: Pop, jazz
- **Recommendation**: ★★★★☆

Great for a relaxed practice atmosphere.

### String Family

#### Acoustic Guitar

Nylon-string guitar tone.

- **Characteristics**: Natural, gentle sound
- **Best For**: Singer-songwriter practice
- **Recommendation**: ★★★☆☆

Perfect for practicing songs you'd sing with guitar.

### Percussion Family

#### Vibraphone

Metallic, resonant tone.

- **Characteristics**: Long sustain
- **Best For**: Pitch perception training
- **Recommendation**: ★★★☆☆

The sustained notes help with pitch matching practice.

#### Marimba

Warm, woody tone.

- **Characteristics**: Clear attack, quick decay
- **Best For**: Rhythm-focused practice
- **Recommendation**: ★★★☆☆

Helps develop tempo awareness.

### Wind Family

#### Flute

Breathy, flowing tone.

- **Characteristics**: Delicate, transparent sound
- **Best For**: Breath control
- **Recommendation**: ★★★★☆

Great for practicing wind instrument-like phrasing.

#### Clarinet

Warm, rounded tone.

- **Characteristics**: Rich mid-range
- **Best For**: Mid-range stability
- **Recommendation**: ★★★☆☆

Useful when focusing on mid-range practice.

### Other

#### Sine Wave

Pure sine wave tone.

- **Characteristics**: No harmonics, fundamental only
- **Best For**: Pure pitch training
- **Recommendation**: ★★★☆☆

For pure pitch verification. Not musical, but ideal for pitch checking.

## Recommendations by Goal

| Practice Goal | Recommended Source |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Basic practice / Not sure | Grand Piano |
| Relaxed atmosphere | Electric Piano |
| Singer-songwriter | Acoustic Guitar |
| Pitch perception | Vibraphone, Sine Wave |
| Breath practice | Flute |
| Tempo awareness | Marimba |

## How to Change Settings

1. Tap "Settings" from the home screen
2. Open "Output Settings" in the "Audio" section
3. Select your preferred sound from "Scale Sound"

## Volume Adjustment

Scale sound volume can be adjusted with the "Scale Playback Volume" slider.

- **Louder**: When you want clear guide tones
- **Quieter**: When you want to focus on your own voice

This is separate from recording playback volume, so you can customize for practice vs. review.

## Summary

- **Grand Piano** is the most versatile when in doubt
- Changing sound sources based on practice goals is effective
- **Flute** for breath practice, **Vibraphone** for pitch training
- Adjust volume to create your optimal practice environment
