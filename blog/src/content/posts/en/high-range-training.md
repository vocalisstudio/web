## Why High Notes Are Difficult

Three main reasons make high notes challenging:

1. **Throat tension**: Straining to produce sound
2. **Weak breath support**: Inadequate diaphragmatic breathing
3. **Overreaching**: Practicing beyond current ability

Use Vocalis Studio to develop your high range gradually.

## Recommended Scale Settings

### For Beginners

![Recording screen](/images/en/02_recording_idle.png)

**Five Tone** is recommended.

- **Scale**: Five Tone
- **Starting Key**: A comfortable note
- **Key Progression**: Ascending only
- **Progression Count**: 3-5
- **Tempo**: 60-80 BPM

With only 5 notes, you can approach high notes in short phrases.

### For Intermediate

Try **Octave Repeat**.

- **Scale**: Octave Repeat
- **Starting Key**: Mid-range
- **Key Progression**: Ascending then Descending
- **Progression Count**: 5-7
- **Tempo**: 70-90 BPM

The top note repeats 4 times, building stability in your upper range.

### For Advanced

Use **Rossini Scale** for serious training.

- **Scale**: Rossini Scale
- **Starting Key**: Lower range
- **Key Progression**: Ascending only
- **Progression Count**: 7-10
- **Tempo**: 80-100 BPM

The 1.5 octave range develops flexibility.

## 5 Tips for High Range Practice

### 1. Always Warm Up

Never start with high notes.

1. Begin with humming in mid-range
2. Gradually expand your range
3. Only attempt high notes when warmed up

### 2. Avoid Tension

Throat tension makes high notes harder.

- **Yawn sensation**: Keep throat open
- **Light production**: Rest when it feels heavy
- **Lip trills**: Release tension

### 3. Focus on Breath Flow

Breath support is crucial for high notes.

- **Diaphragmatic breathing**: Support from your core
- **Consistent airflow**: Don't let it break
- **Gentle breath**: Don't push too hard

### 4. Raise Range Gradually

![Pitch graph](/images/en/04_pitch_graph_fullscreen.png)

Don't jump to high notes suddenly—increase by semitones.

**Key Progression Settings**:
- **Step Interval**: 1 (semitone increments)
- **Progression Count**: Start with fewer
- **Check recordings**: Review accuracy in statistics

### 5. Take Breaks

High range practice strains your voice.

- **15 minutes practice → 5 minutes rest**
- Stop immediately if discomfort occurs
- Daily short sessions are most effective

## Using the Pitch Graph

The pitch graph is essential for high range practice.

### What to Check

1. **Pitch stability**: Can you hold high notes steadily?
2. **Entry accuracy**: Do you reach target pitch quickly?
3. **Voice quality**: Is your production strained?

### Improvement Tips

**If pitch is unstable**:
- Slow the tempo
- Lower the range
- Practice short phrases with Five Tone

**If voice quality deteriorates on high notes**:
- Review your technique
- Focus on breath support
- Return to lower range

## Using Statistics

![Statistics screen](/images/en/05_statistics_pitch.png)

Track your high range progress with statistics.

### Key Metrics

- **Average pitch accuracy**: Aim for 80%+
- **Vibrato stability**: Is it regular even on high notes?
- **Pitch range**: Your stable production range

### Tracking Progress

1. Record regularly with same settings
2. Compare statistics
3. Raise the key when accuracy improves

## Important Notes

### Don't Overdo It

- Stop immediately if you feel pain
- Rest when your voice is hoarse
- Limit daily practice to 15-20 minutes

### Think Long-Term

High range development takes time.

- Check progress weekly
- Be patient and consistent
- Keep records for comparison

## Summary

- Start with **Five Tone** and increase difficulty gradually
- Relaxed production and breath support are fundamental
- Check objectively with pitch graph and statistics
- Don't strain—continue with short daily sessions
