## 9 Scale Patterns Available

Vocalis Studio offers 9 different scale patterns designed for various practice goals.

![Recording screen scale settings](/images/en/02_recording_idle.png)

Let's explore each pattern's characteristics and ideal use cases.

## Basic Scales

### Five Tone

The most fundamental scale pattern.

**Structure**: Do→Re→Mi→Fa→Sol→Fa→Mi→Re→Do (9 notes)

A familiar ascending-then-descending pattern. Perfect for warm-ups and foundational practice.

**Best For**:
- Daily warm-ups
- Pitch awareness training
- Beginners' foundation work

### Five Tone Down

A simple descending-only pattern.

**Structure**: Sol→Fa→Mi→Re→Do (5 notes)

Starting from a higher note allows for natural voice flow.

**Best For**:
- Descending phrase practice
- Relaxed vocalization
- Tension release training

## Octave Scales

### Octave Repeat

A pattern that repeats the top note 4 times.

**Structure**: Do→Mi→Sol→High Do→High Do→High Do→High Do→Sol→Mi→Do (10 notes)

Singing the highest note multiple times builds high-range stability.

**Best For**:
- High note stabilization
- Long tone alternative
- Endurance training

### Octave Long Tone

A pattern with an extended hold at the octave.

**Structure**: Do→Mi→Sol→High Do (4-beat hold)→Sol→Mi→Do

Holding the top note develops breath control and pitch maintenance simultaneously.

**Best For**:
- Breath control
- Pitch stability
- Vibrato practice

## Arpeggio Scales

### Broken Scale

An arpeggio pattern with interval leaps.

**Structure**: Do→Sol→Mi→High Do→Sol→Mi→Do (7 notes)

The jumping intervals require more precise pitch control.

**Best For**:
- Interval leap practice
- Pitch accuracy improvement
- Intermediate level advancement

### Broken Scale Double

The Broken Scale repeated twice.

**Structure**: Broken Scale × 2 (13 notes)

Longer phrases build endurance and concentration.

**Best For**:
- Extended phrase practice
- Stamina building
- Concentration training

### Arpeggio Down Triple

A comprehensive 16-note arpeggio pattern.

A descending arpeggio repeated three times—designed for serious vocal training.

**Best For**:
- Professional vocal training
- Full-range equalization
- Advanced comprehensive practice

## Extended Scale

### Rossini Scale

An advanced scale spanning 1.5 octaves.

**Structure**: Ascends one octave, then continues up a fifth before descending (13 notes)

A professional pattern used even in opera training. Highly effective for high-range development.

**Best For**:
- Range extension
- High-range development
- Advanced challenges

## Free Recording

### Off (No Scale)

Record without any scale accompaniment.

Use this when you want to sing at your own pace or practice song phrases.

**Best For**:
- Song phrase practice
- Free vocalization
- Rhythm-free practice

## Recommended Scales by Goal

| Practice Goal | Recommended Scale |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Warm-up | Five Tone |
| High note stability | Octave Repeat, Octave Long Tone |
| Pitch accuracy | Broken Scale |
| Range extension | Rossini Scale |
| Endurance | Broken Scale Double, Arpeggio Down Triple |
| Relaxation | Five Tone Down |

## Scale Selection Tips

### For Beginners

Start with **Five Tone**. Once comfortable, gradually challenge your high range with **Octave Repeat**.

### For Intermediate Singers

Use **Broken Scale** to master interval leaps, and **Octave Long Tone** to refine pitch stability.

### For Advanced Singers

Train your entire range evenly with **Rossini Scale** and **Arpeggio Down Triple**.

## Summary

- Use **Five Tone** for basic warm-ups
- Use **Octave** scales for high-range strengthening
- Use **Broken Scale** for pitch precision
- Use **Rossini Scale** for range extension
- Choosing the right scale for your goal is the key to improvement
