Your First Recording

Guide

Introduction

Recording your voice is the first step toward understanding and improving your singing. Vocalis Studio makes this process simple and straightforward.

As the developer of this app, I designed the recording flow to be as intuitive as possible. You can start recording within seconds of opening the app.

In this guide, I will walk you through the basic recording process.

Summary

Making your first recording takes just three steps:

  1. Tap “Start Recording” on the home screen
  2. Set the scale type to “Off” for free recording
  3. Tap the record button to start, and tap again to stop

Basic Steps

Step 1: Open the Recording Screen

From the home screen, tap the “Start Recording” button. This takes you to the recording screen where you can configure your recording settings.

Step 2: Configure Settings (Optional)

For your first recording, I recommend keeping things simple:

  • Scale Type: Set to “Off” for free recording without a reference scale
  • Other Settings: You can leave these at their defaults for now

The settings panel is on the left side in landscape mode, or can be toggled in portrait mode.

Step 3: Start Recording

Tap the red record button to begin. A brief countdown will appear, then recording starts automatically.

While recording, you will see:

  • Real-time waveform: Visual representation of your voice
  • Audio level meter: Shows your current volume
  • Pitch detection: Displays the detected pitch in real-time

When you are finished, tap the stop button. Your recording is automatically saved.

What Happens After Recording

Once you stop recording, you have several options:

  • Play Last Recording: Listen to what you just recorded
  • Go to Analysis: View detailed pitch analysis and statistics
  • Start New Recording: Begin another recording session

The analysis screen provides valuable insights into your pitch accuracy and vocal characteristics.

Common Questions

Q: How long can I record?

Free users can record up to 30 seconds per session, with 5 recordings per day. Premium subscribers have extended limits.

Q: Where are my recordings saved?

All recordings are saved locally on your device. You can access them from the “Recording List” on the home screen.

Q: Do I need headphones?

Headphones are recommended but not required. Using headphones can improve pitch detection accuracy by preventing feedback from the speaker.

Summary

  • Recording is simple: just tap and sing
  • Start with “Scale Off” mode for free recording
  • Real-time feedback helps you monitor your voice
  • Recordings are saved automatically for later review

In the next guide, we will explore how to use scale practice mode to improve your pitch accuracy.
